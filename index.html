What is personal data?

How we define it

Personal data is any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual. This includes your name, your device's unique identifiers, your gameplay statistics, and any other data that could reasonably be used to identify you — whether on its own or combined with other data.

Data we have aggregated and anonymised so that no individual can be identified is not personal data for the purposes of this policy.

Let's Play is a trivia and quiz application available on iOS. This policy covers data we handle when you use our app, visit any associated website, or contact us for support.

Your privacy rights

What you can ask us to do

Regardless of where you live, you have the right to know, access, correct, transfer, restrict the processing of, and delete your personal data. Exercising these rights will never result in you receiving a worse experience in the app.

Access

Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.

Correction

Ask us to fix inaccurate or incomplete data.

Deletion

Request that we erase your account and associated data.

Portability

Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.

To make a request, email us at privacy@letsplay.app. We will respond within 30 days. Where we cannot fulfil a request — for example, where deletion would conflict with a legal obligation — we will explain why.

Data we collect from you

What we gather and when

We strive to collect only the data we genuinely need to provide the game and improve it. Below is what we may collect depending on how you use the app.

Account & profile data

  • Username or display name — shown to other players in multiplayer sessions
  • Email address — used to create and recover your account
  • Profile avatar — if you choose to set one

Gameplay data

  • Quiz answers and scores — to generate your results, leaderboard position, and history
  • Category preferences — Music, Sport & Leisure, Film & TV, Arts & Literature, etc. — to personalise suggested categories
  • Session timestamps — when you start and finish a game
  • Multiplayer room codes — to connect you with other players in created or joined games

Device & technical data

  • Device model and iOS version — for troubleshooting and compatibility
  • App version — to confirm you are on a supported release
  • Crash and diagnostic logs — to identify and fix bugs
  • IP address — collected transiently for fraud prevention and network communication
We do not collect your precise location, contacts, camera, microphone, or any sensitive personal data unless you explicitly grant permission for a feature that requires it.

Data from other sources

Third parties who may share data with us

We may receive data about you from the following sources:

  • Sign in with Apple — if you choose this login method, Apple shares a verified email address (or a private relay address) with us
  • Analytics providers — aggregated, non-identifiable usage metrics only
  • Crash reporting services — anonymised stack traces and device context to help us fix bugs

We do not purchase data from data brokers or receive personal data from advertisers.

How we use your data

Our purposes and legal bases

We use your data only when we have a valid legal basis to do so: your consent, the necessity to fulfil our contract with you (providing the game), a legitimate interest (improving and securing the app), or a legal obligation.

Run the game Serve multiplayer Fix bugs Secure your account Send transactional emails Comply with law
  • Powering the game — displaying questions, recording answers, calculating scores, and showing results screens
  • Multiplayer functionality — matching you with a room you created or joined, synchronising question delivery, and sharing scores between participants
  • Personalisation — remembering which categories you play most to surface them prominently (opt-out available in Settings)
  • App improvement — analysing anonymised crash and usage data to prioritise fixes and new features
  • Security and fraud prevention — detecting abuse, cheating, and unauthorised access
  • Communications — sending you emails about your account, password resets, and material changes to this policy
We do not use automated profiling to make decisions that significantly affect you. Leaderboard ranks are computed mechanically from scores, not from personal attributes.

How we share your data

Who else sees it

We do not sell your personal data. We do not share it with third parties for their own marketing purposes. We may share data in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers — companies that help us operate the app (cloud hosting, analytics, crash reporting) under strict data-processing agreements that prohibit them from using your data for any other purpose
  • Other players — your username and score are visible to other participants in the same multiplayer room; your full email is never shared with other players
  • Legal requirements — where disclosure is required by law, a court order, or to protect the safety of users or the public
  • Business transfer — in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data would transfer to the new entity under the same privacy protections; we will notify you in advance

Security

How we protect your information

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the nature and sensitivity of the data we hold. These include:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher) and at rest
  • Passwords stored as salted hashes — never in plain text
  • Access to production systems limited to authorised personnel only
  • Regular security reviews and dependency audits

No system is perfectly secure. If you believe your account has been compromised, please contact us immediately at security@letsplay.app.

Children

Players under 13

Let's Play is a family-friendly trivia game. We define a child as anyone under the age of 13, or the minimum age required by applicable law in your jurisdiction.

We do not knowingly collect personal data from children without verifiable parental consent. If you are a parent and believe your child has created an account without your permission, please email privacy@letsplay.app and we will delete the account and associated data promptly.

Children's accounts created with a parent or guardian's supervision are welcome. The parent's email address will be used for all account communications.

Multiplayer & online features

What's shared in a live game

When you Create Multiplayer or Join Multiplayer, the app connects to our real-time game server. The following data is exchanged during an active session:

  • Your display name — visible to all players in the room
  • Your answers and score — broadcast to all participants at the end of each question
  • Room code — a short-lived identifier; expired codes cannot be used to retrieve historic session data

Session data on our servers is purged within 24 hours of the game ending. We do not retain a record of who played with whom beyond the anonymised aggregate statistics used to improve question difficulty.

Data retention

How long we keep your data

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law. Specific retention periods:

Account data

Kept until you delete your account, then purged within 30 days.

Gameplay history

Retained for 12 months to power your personal stats screen.

Crash logs

Automatically deleted after 90 days.

Multiplayer sessions

Purged within 24 hours of session end.

International transfers

Where your data is processed

Our servers are currently located in Canada and the United States. If you are based in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or another jurisdiction with data-transfer restrictions, your data may be transferred internationally.

We rely on Standard Contractual Clauses and other approved transfer mechanisms to ensure your data receives the same level of protection regardless of where it is processed. By using Let's Play, you acknowledge this international processing.
